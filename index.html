🧠 What is a Brain-Computer Interface?

A Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) creates a direct communication pathway between the brain and an external device. BCIs are helping people regain movement, communicate without speaking, and may completely transform the future of human-computer interaction.

How Does a BCI Work?

🧠
💻

Brain signals are detected, decoded using AI algorithms, and translated into commands that computers understand.

Myth vs Fact

❌ MYTH

BCIs can read every thought in your brain.

Click Me 👆

✅ FACT

Current BCIs decode specific neural signals, not every thought or memory.

❌ MYTH

BCIs are only used for mind control.

Click Me 👆

✅ FACT

Most BCIs are designed to restore communication, movement, or assist people with disabilities.

History of Brain-Computer Interfaces

1924

Hans Berger records the first human EEG.

1973

The term "Brain-Computer Interface" begins appearing in research.

2004

BCIs begin helping people communicate using only brain signals.

2024

Neuralink begins early human clinical trials.

Future

Safe, ethical BCIs could transform medicine, accessibility, and communication.
